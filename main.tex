\textbf{question(iv): }\\
Show that $\vec{A} + \vec{Aâ€™}$ is symmetric matrix.if
\begin{align}
     \vec{A} = \myvec{2 &  4 \\ 3 & 5}
\end{align}

 

\textbf{solution: }\\
given,
\begin{align}
\vec{A} &= \myvec{2 &  4 \\ 3 & 5}\\
\implies
\vec{A'} &=\myvec{2 &  3 \\ 4 & 5}\\
\vec{A}+\vec{A'} &= \myvec{2 &  4 \\ 3 & 5}+\myvec{2 &  3 \\ 4 & 5}\\
\implies
\vec{A}+\vec{A'} &= \myvec{2+2 &  4+3 \\ 3+4 & 5+5}\\
\implies
\vec{A}+\vec{A'} &= \myvec{4 &  7 \\ 7 & 10}
\end{align}
$\therefore$ $\vec{A}+\vec{A'}$ \text{ is symmetric matrix}
